Managing Partner

Chief Ganiu Adekale Kelani ESQ.

Chief Ganiu Adekale Kelani, (Ex-Chief Magistrate of Lagos State) is the Founder and Principal Partner of G. Ade. Kelani Chambers. He attended both Holborn College of Law ETC and College of Law in the United Kingdom from 1962 – 1968. He holds an L.L.B (Hons) Degree of the University of London (1st August, 1968). He was admitted as a member of The Honourable Society of Middle Temple from 1964-1969. ■ Chief Ganiu Adekale Kelani, was admitted to the Nigerian Bar in November, 1970 and is a member of the Nigerian Bar Association (NBA). He was in active private legal practice from 1970-1976. He was appointed as a Magistrate of Lagos State, Grade II in June, 1976 and progressively rose to the position of Chief Magistrate of Lagos State Grade 1 in December, 1983. He retired as Chief Magistrate of Lagos state in September, 1994. ■ Chief Ganiu Adekale Kelani heads the Litigation Group in the firm. He has been in very active litigation practice and also acted as counsel in a number of arbitral proceedings. The scope of his practice covers commercial and public law, advisory work, drafting, litigation and dispute resolution through negotiation and mediation

Senior Partner

Bayo Orekoya ESQ.

Bayo Orekoya, Esq. is an alumnus of the University of Lagos, Nigeria (UNILAG) where he bagged his Bachelor of Law (LL.B) degree with Honours in 2006 and was thereafter called to the prestigious Nigerian Bar in 2007 as a Barrister and Solicitor of the Supreme Court of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. He commenced his Legal career in the Commercial Law Office of Treasure Field Chambers as an Associate member. His sound understanding of Corporate/Commercial Law practice together with the excellent and dutiful performance in the discharge of assignments within a short time line made him to become the Head of Chambers in 2008. The position which he held until June, 2009 before moving to the Law Firm of Messrs F. O. Kilani & Co. (Solicitors & Consultants). At Messrs F. O. Kilani & Co. he is the head, Capital Market Department where he manages corporate clients (Stock broking firms/Issuing Houses) on their duties to the various statutory regulatory bodies such as the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) and individual investors on the legal implications of their investment proposal at the Capital Market. Due to his legal prowess, he was drafted to join forces with the Real Estate Investment Department of the Firm and also rose to become the Head, Real Estate/Property Investment Department. Whilst in Messrs F. O. Kilani & Co. Barr. Bayo Orekoya is an Associate of the Portfolio Management Institute, Nigeria, Member of the Nigerian Bar Association (NBA), International Bar Association (IBA); he is an accredited agent of the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) and a licensed Capital Market Solicitor of the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). He has participated in various municipal and international seminars, conferences, trainings and workshops on his area of core competences. He is a Managing Partner of the firm

Afolabi Dawodu ESQ.

Mr Afolabi Dawodu graduated from the University of Lagos, Akoka, Lagos, Nigeria in 2006 and was called to the Nigerian Bar in 2007. His area of specialization is commercial law, intellectual property law, general solicitors practice and litigation. He is an astute negotiator in commercial transactions.
Mr. Dawodu has been involved in numerous litigations at the Lagos State High Courts, Oyo State High Court, Akwa Ibom State High Court, Rivers State High Court, Federal High Court, (Lagos and Abeokuta) Court of Appeal (Lagos and Ibadan) and the Supreme Court (Abuja). He has been involved in academic projects such as the publication of the Nigerian Intellectual Property Law Reports published by Alexander Payne Ltd for which he served as a co-editor and also co-authored the ‘Handbook on the Law of Agency’, a publication of the Taslim Elias Chambers of the Faculty of Law, University of Lagos.
As part of his duties for the law firms where he has served, Mr. Afolabi Dawodu has acted for many years as company secretary and legal adviser to various high net worth companies.
He is a member of the Nigerian Bar Association, and is highly proficient in Information Technology. Mr. Dawodu has since been in active legal practice since 2007. He is a Senior Associate.
